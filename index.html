HOW TO USE
Tap each phase to expand. ● Green = responses allowed. ● Red = no responses.
①
Beginning Phase
▼
Untap Step
Untap ALL your permanents. Nobody can cast spells or activate abilities.
✕ No responses
Upkeep Step
"At the beginning of your upkeep" effects trigger here. Everyone may respond.
💡 Last chance to act before you draw your card.
✓ Priority open
Draw Step
Draw 1 card. Everyone may respond after.
✓ Priority open
②
Pre-Combat Main Phase
▼
Play a Land (optional)
One land per turn, from your hand. Lands are not spells — they cannot be countered.
💡 Only during YOUR main phase with an empty stack.
Cast Spells
Cast creatures, sorceries, instants, artifacts, enchantments, planeswalkers. Pay the mana cost shown top-right.
💡 Mana empties at end of phase — use it or lose it!
✓ Priority open
③
Combat Phase
▼
Beginning of Combat
"At the beginning of combat" triggers fire. Before attackers are chosen.
💡 Last chance to tap opponent's creatures before they can attack!
✓ Priority open
Declare Attackers
Choose which creatures attack and who they target. Attackers tap unless they have Vigilance. In Commander you can attack different opponents with different creatures!
✓ Priority opens after
Declare Blockers
Defending players choose blockers. Blockers do NOT tap. If multiple creatures block one attacker, the attacking player chooses the damage order.
💡 Each creature can only block one attacker.
✓ Priority opens after
First Strike Damage (if any)
Only if any creature has First Strike or Double Strike. They deal damage before everyone else.
✓ Priority opens after
Combat Damage
All remaining creatures deal damage simultaneously. Creatures with lethal damage die via state-based actions.
💡 Both creatures can kill each other at the same time — damage is simultaneous!
✓ Priority opens after
End of Combat
"At end of combat" effects trigger. Creatures leave combat.
✓ Priority open
④
Post-Combat Main Phase
▼
Cast More Spells
Same as the first main phase. Play a land if you haven't yet.
💡 Often better to cast big creatures AFTER combat — opponents can't kill them before they attack.
✓ Priority open
⑤
Ending Phase
▼
End Step
"At the beginning of the end step" triggers fire. Last chance for everyone to cast instants.
✓ Priority open
Cleanup Step
Discard to 7 cards. Remove all damage from creatures. End "until end of turn" effects. Pass the turn.
✕ No responses normally
⚜ Commander Rules
Commander Damage: If one commander deals 21+ combat damage to you, you lose — regardless of life total. Tracked per commander.
Commander Tax: Each recast from the command zone costs +{2}. Third cast costs +4 extra mana total.
Zone Replacement: When your commander would go to graveyard or exile, you may redirect it to the command zone instead.
Starting Life: 40 life in Commander (not 20). Poison still kills at 10 counters.
🃏 Card Types
When can you play each type of card? Here's a quick reference:
🐉
Creature
▼
When to play
Only during YOUR main phase, with the stack empty. Creatures enter the battlefield and stay there as permanents.
💡 Subject to Summoning Sickness — cannot attack or use {T} abilities the turn they enter unless they have Haste.
How they work
Creatures have Power/Toughness shown as X/X in the bottom right. Power = damage they deal. Toughness = damage needed to kill them. They can attack, block, and use activated abilities.
⚡
Instant
▼
When to play
ANY time you have priority — your turn, opponent's turn, during combat, even in response to other spells. The most flexible card type in the game.
💡 Holding up mana during your opponent's turn signals you might have an instant. This is called "playing around" removal.
How they work
Cast the spell, pay the cost, it resolves and goes to the graveyard. One-time effect. Does not stay on the battlefield.
📜
Sorcery
▼
When to play
Only during YOUR main phase, with the stack empty. Unlike Instants, you cannot cast Sorceries in response to anything or on an opponent's turn.
💡 Think of Sorceries as "slow instants" — same one-time effect, but you have to plan when to use them carefully.
How they work
Cast, pay the cost, it resolves and goes to the graveyard. One-time effect. Some of the most powerful effects in the game are Sorceries (board wipes, big draw spells).
🏔
Land
▼
When to play
ONE land per turn, during YOUR main phase, with the stack empty. Lands are NOT spells — they cannot be countered and do not go on the stack.
💡 Playing a land every turn is one of the most important habits in MTG. Missing a land drop puts you behind on mana.
How they work
Tap a land to add mana of its colour to your pool. Basic lands produce one colour each: Plains=W, Island=U, Swamp=B, Mountain=R, Forest=G. Mana empties at end of each phase.
⚙
Artifact
▼
When to play
During YOUR main phase with the stack empty (unless they have Flash). Artifacts are permanents — they stay on the battlefield.
How they work
Colourless by default — can be cast with any mana. Includes Equipment (attach to creatures for bonuses), Vehicles (crew them to make them creatures), and utility rocks (like Sol Ring which produces extra mana).
💡 Equipment stays on the battlefield even if the creature it's attached to dies. You can reattach it to another creature.
✨
Enchantment
▼
When to play
During YOUR main phase with the stack empty (unless they have Flash). Enchantments are permanents that stay on the battlefield and provide ongoing effects.
How they work
Global enchantments affect the whole game. Auras attach to a specific permanent (creature, land, etc.) and give it bonuses or penalties. If the permanent an Aura is attached to leaves the battlefield, the Aura goes to the graveyard.
💡 Be careful enchanting opponents' creatures — if they sacrifice it, your Aura dies with it.
🌟
Planeswalker
▼
When to play
During YOUR main phase with the stack empty. Planeswalkers are powerful permanents that represent allies fighting alongside you.
How they work
Enter with a set number of loyalty counters. Once per turn during your main phase, activate ONE loyalty ability — either adding or removing counters. Opponents can attack planeswalkers directly instead of attacking you. If loyalty reaches 0, the planeswalker dies.
💡 You can only use ONE loyalty ability per planeswalker per turn, even if you have enough counters for more.
⚔
Battle
▼
When to play
During YOUR main phase with the stack empty. Battles are a newer card type introduced in 2023.
How they work
Siege battles (the most common type) enter with defence counters and are controlled by you but protected by an opponent. Players can attack the battle to remove defence counters. When all counters are removed, the battle is defeated and exiles — transforming into a powerful card for the player who defeated it.
💡 You can attack your OWN battle to defeat it yourself and get the reward.
